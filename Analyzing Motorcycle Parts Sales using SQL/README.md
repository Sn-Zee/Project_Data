<h1 align="center"> Analyzing Motorcycle Parts Sales Using SQL </h1>

## Project Overview

This project explores motorcylce parts sales data using SQL to uncover trends in sales performance and generate actionable business insights. The aanlysis examine sales transactions from **June to August 2021**, focusing on key business dimensions such as warehouse, product line, order value, quantity sold, client type, and payment method.

This primary method objective is to evaluate the sales performance of product lines and warehouses by analyzing **net revenue** (total sales minus payment fees). Moreover, the original business problem require the calculation of wholesale net revenue by product line, warehouse, and month, the project extends the analysis to identify the highest- and lowest-performing warehouses, determine the best and worst-performing product lines, examine customer distribution by client type, and analyze payment method preferences and product popularity.

Through exploratory data analysis (EDA), this project demonstrates practical SQL skills by transforming raw transactional data into meaningful insights that can support data-driven business decisions, improve sales performance evaluation, and provide deeper understanding of customer purchasing patterns.

## Objectives

- Analyze motorcycle parts sales data from June to August 2021.
- Evaluate warehouse and product line performce based on net revenue.
- Identify sales trends, customer distribution, payment preferences, and product popularity.
- Apply SQL techniques to perform exploratory data analysis and answer key business questions.
- Generate actionable insights to support data-driven business decisions.

## Dataset Information


| Column | Data Type | Description |
| ------ | ----------| ----------- |
| `order_number` | `VARCHAR` | Unique order number. |
| `date ` | `DATE` | Date of the order, from June to August 2021. |
| `warehouse` | `VARCHAR` | The warehouse that the order was made from - `North`, `Central`, or `West`. |
| `client_type` | `VARCHAR` | Whether the order was `Retail` or `Wholesale`. |
| `product_line` | `VARCHAR` | Type of product ordered. |
| `quantity` | `INT` | Number of products order. |
| `unit_price` | `FLOAT` | Price per product (dollars). |
| `total` | `FLOAT` | Total price of the order (dollars). |
| `payment` | `VARCHAR` | Payment method - `Credit card`, `Transfer`, or `Cash`. |
| `payment_fee` | `FLOAT` | Percentage of `total` charged as a result of the `payment` method |

## Methodology

The analysis was performed using SQL in PostgreSQL following an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) approach. Queries were written using aggragation functions, Common Table Expressions (CTEs), window functions, and subqueries to summarize sales performance, rank product performance, identify trends, and compare customer purchasing behavior.

## SQL Techniques Applied

- Aggregation Functions
- GROUP BY
- ORDER BY
- WINDOW Functions
- Common Table Expressions
- Aliasing
- Joins
- Subqueries

## Questions Answered

1. How much **Wholesale** net revenue each product line generated by **warehouse** and **per month**?
2. Which warehouse generated the highest and lowest net revenue?
3. Which product lines generated the highest net revenue within each warehouse?
4. Which product lines generated the lowest net revenue within each warehouse?
5. Which product lines generated the highest and lowest net revenue across **all warehouse**?
6. Which product lines received the highest and lowest number of orders within **each warehouse**?
7. Which product line received the highest and lowest number of orders across all warehouses?
8. How are **Retail** and **Wholesale** orders distributed across each warehouse?
9. Which payment methods are most frequently used by each client type?
10. How did warehouse and product line performance change from June to August 2021?

## Key Insights

1. The analysis of wholesale net revenue by product line, warehouse and month shows that **Engine** product line recorded the highest single-month wholesale net revenue in the dataset, reaching 9,528.71 in the **Central** warehouse in the month of **August**. Additionally, **Frame & Body** product line has consistently perfomred well for the **Central** and **North** warehouse. This shows strong customer demand for this product line.

2. This analysis of warehouses net revenue shows that **Central** warehouse has generated the highest revenue across all warehouses, totaling to **141,972.13**. On the other hand, West generated the lowest net revenue at **46,922.59**. This indicates that Central warehouse contributed most to overall sales during the three-month period. Identifying and prioritizing the best-performing product line in each warehouse can help optimize invetory allocation and warehouse planning to improve revenue, particularly for the West warehouse.

3. This analysis of product line performance by warehouse has identified that **Suspension & traction** generated the highest net revenue in both **Central (32,668.77)** and **North (30,112.98)** warehouses. In contrast, **Frame & body** was the top-performing product line in **West** warehouse, generating **10,585.68** of net revenue. These results highlight differences in customer demand across warehouses, suggesting that inventory allocation and promotional efforts should be tailored to each warehouse's highest-perfoming product lines to maximize sales.

4. The analysis revealed that the lowest-performing product line in both **North** and **West** warehouses was Engine product line, generating **7,236.35** and **3,445.27** respectively. In contrast, **Miscellanous**  was the lowest-performing product line in the **Central** warehouse, generating net revenue of **12,219.69**.The results suggests that the lowest-performing product lines warrant further analysis to identify the factors contributing to their lower revenue performance.

5. This analysis of product line net revenue indicated that **Suspension & traction** produced the highest net revenue across all warehouses, totaling **73,009.11**. Whereas, the **Miscellaneous** product line generated lowest in net revenue. In which, **Suspension & traction** has generated approximately **2.7 times** the revenue of **Miscellaneous** Comparing the product line performance enables the business to identify revenue-driving products while highlighting lower-performing categories that may require further evaluation of other possible factors.

6. The analysis of product line order quantities by warehouse revealed that **Braking System** was the most ordered product line in both **Central (1,024)** and **West (501)** warehouses. In contrast, **Suspension & traction** received the highest number of orders in the North warehouse, totaling to 866. Further analysis revealed that the **Engine** product line recorded the fewest orders across all warehouses. Despite its lower order volume, the Engine product line had an average unit price of approximately 60 across the three warehouses, suggesting that product price may be one of the several factors influencing purchasing behavior.

7. The analysis revealed that the **Suspension & traction** product line received the highest number of orders **(2,145)** across all warehouses. It also generated the highest net revenue **(73,009.11)** and was identified as top revenue-generating product, demonstrating consistently strong performance. Additionally, resulsts show that **Braking System** and **Suspension & traction** have almost similar total quantity sold, but **Suspension & traction** generated nearly twice the net revenue. In contrast, although the **Engine** product line recorded the fewest orders, it was not the lowest-performing product line in terms of net revenue, suggesting that order volume alone does not determine overall revenue perfromance. 

8. The analysis revealed tat **Retail** orders accounted for the majority of transactions across all warehouses, indicating that Retail customers comprised the primary source of sales. Additionally, the **Central** warehouse recorded the highest number of both **Retail (371)** and **Wholesale (109)** orders, while the **West** warehouse had the lowest order counts across all thre warehouses. 

9. The analysis uncovered that **Retail** customers overwhelmingly preferred **Credit Card** payments method, accounting for **659** orders, or approximately 66% of all transcations during the three-month period. It was also revealed that **Retail** customers primarly used **Credit Card** payments, **Wholesale** customers excusively use **Transfer**. Because Credit Card transactions incur a payment processing fee, their widespread use contributes more to total payment fees than other payment methods.

10. 
    **Central Warehouse**
        
        The analysis revealed that only the Engine and Frame & body product lines recorded positive revenue growth from July to August in the Central warehouse. Despite experiencing a decline between June and July, both product lines rebounded strongly in August, reaching their highest monthly net revenues during the three-month period.

    **North Warehouse**
        
        In the North warehouse, Braking system and Miscellaneous finished the period with declining revenue trends. Converesly, the Electrical system product line recorded the highest month-to-month increase between July and August, indicating a rebound in sales.

    **West Warehouse**

        The West warehouse exhibitied mixed performance across product lines. While Engine recordedno sales in Augsut, Suspension & Traction maintained relatively stable revenue throughout the three-month period and finished with positive growth. In contrast, Frame & body and  Miscellaneous experienced continuous declines in monthly in monthly net revenue from June throughout August.

    Overall, the trend analysis highlights the month-to-month performance of each product line across the three warehouse, emphasizing the importance of continuously monitoring sales trends to support inventory planning, promotions strategies, and timely business decisions.
